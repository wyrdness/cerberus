# Sources & References

## Ancient Greek Sources

### Primary Texts
- Homer (~8th century BCE). *The Iliad*. Epic containing references to underworld guardians.
- Homer (~8th century BCE). *The Odyssey*, Book 11. Odysseus' journey to the underworld.
- Hesiod (~8th century BCE). *Theogony*. Genealogy of gods and monsters, including Cerberus' parentage.
- Apollodorus (~2nd century CE). *Bibliotheca* (Library). Detailed accounts of Greek myths including Hercules' twelve labors.

### Roman Sources
- Virgil (1st century BCE). *Aeneid*, Book 6. Detailed account of Aeneas entering the underworld with the Sibyl and encountering Cerberus.
- Ovid (1st century BCE-1st century CE). *Metamorphoses*. Roman retelling of Greek myths.
- Seneca (1st century CE). *Hercules Furens*. Roman tragedy featuring Hercules and Cerberus.

## Medieval & Renaissance

### Dante Alighieri
- Dante Alighieri (~1320). *Divine Comedy: Inferno*, Canto VI. Cerberus as guardian of the third circle of Hell.
- Commentary and scholarly analysis of Dante's use of classical mythology
